Scholarships.com: College Search

Background

Previous design had over 100 data points and no graphs leading to data overload and user fatigue.

Assignment

To enhance the user experience for the college search section by providing meaningful information concisely leading to higher SEO ranking.

Design

Crafted a card based layout showcasing key data for each institution on the college search home page. In the college detail page, expand upon the key data points with more related and helpful information along with the use of unique and friendly graphs.

Collaboration

Worked closely with the leadership to reach a consensus on college data to be displayed. Also worked closely with the other engineers when making changes to the business logic and service layer.

Focus

Focused on trimming the number of data points and adding depth and context with graphical representation.

Innovation

Used a drop-down approach to display in-state, out-of-state, in-district tuition costs. Added a CTA to student loans page within the cost card.

Graphics

Designed innovative and unique graphs to keep users interested. Designed and curated intuitive icons to match with each of the key data points.

  • Data Transformation
  • UI/UX
  • Implementation

Scholarships.com: Blog

Background

Previous design had a dated look with limited filter and search capabilities.

Assignment

To modernize the blog section making users more likely to return and share the blog posts leading to higher SEO ranking.

Design

Redesigned the user flow and crafted a fresh card based design with easy to use search, filter, and share functionality.

Collaboration

Worked with other engineers when re-writing the MVC controller and when making updates to the service layer.

Focus

Focused on making the section approachable and easy to use.

Innovation

Designed a featured section to promote the most recent blog post. Introduced the main categories on blog home - latest, popular, and recommended. Added a sharing utility to simplify the process of sharing on social media. Conceptualized the use of hashtags for each post.

Graphics

Incorporated a blog image for each blog post in the card layout and added a default image when none is returned from the database.
